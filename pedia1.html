Cognitive Development

What the Newborn Can Do

  • Recognize face, voice, smell (especially mother)
  • Differentiate patterns, colors (red & black), basic shapes
  • Prefers mother's voice — turns toward it (recognition memory)

⭐ Habituation

  • ↓ response to repeated stimuli
  • ↑ attention to novel stimuli
  • Ability to discriminate between 2 stimuli
  • Self-regulation function (protective)

Emotional Development

💚
Secure Attachment — Formed when parent CONSISTENTLY attends to the infant's urgent needs. Leads to: less crying at 1 year, less aggressive behavior at 2 years. You CANNOT spoil a neonate.

Principles of Physical Development

Cephalocaudal

Head → Toe (downward progression)
Example: head control before walking

Proximodistal

Midline → Periphery (outward)
Example: arm movements before finger control

Neonatal Posture & Movement

  • Posture: semi-flexed position
  • Limb movements: uncontrollable writhing, purposeless hand opening/closing
  • Sleep: evenly distributed throughout 24-hour day

Newborn Red Flags (Go to ER!)

  • Refusal to feed / poor suck → risk for hypoglycemia
  • No spontaneous movement / poor activity
  • Fast breathing / difficulty breathing
  • Coughing
  • Vomiting (esp. bilious or bloody)
  • Fever or low body temperature
  • Severe jaundice / yellowing
  • Watery or acholic (pale) stool

Developmental Surveillance vs Screening

Surveillance (Every Visit)

  • Observation, history, documentation — NO standardized tool
  • Analogy: checking temp with back of hand
  • Done at every well-child visit

Screening (Specific Ages)

  • Standardized validated tool (PEDS, M-CHAT-R)
  • Analogy: using a thermometer
  • Ages: 9, 18, 24 (or 30) months → then yearly
  • Also done anytime parent has concerns
⭐
If child FAILS a screening test → NOT immediately diagnosed → refer for comprehensive developmental evaluation. Screening only detects RISK.

Breastfeeding

💚
WHO/PPS Recommendation: Exclusive breastfeeding until 6 months, continued breastfeeding up to 2 years and beyond (after introduction of complementary feeding).

Benefits for Baby

  • Antibodies — immune protection
  • DHA → brain development
  • Prevents: SIDS, URTI, diarrhea, asthma, eczema, obesity, T1DM, T2DM, leukemia, tooth decay

Benefits for Mother

  • ↓ Cancer risk (breast, uterus, ovaries)
  • ↓ Postpartum depression
  • ↓ Postpartum hemorrhage
  • Promotes return to pre-pregnancy weight

Breastfeeding Technique

  • Position: face-to-face, chest-to-chest, tummy-to-tummy (straight line)
  • Latch: entire nipple + 1 inch of surrounding areola; chin touches breast, lower lip turned outward
  • Duration: 15–30 minutes per breast (extracts foremilk AND hindmilk)
  • Frequency: 8–10 times or more per day (per demand)
  • Watch for hunger cues: mouth opening, head turning, hand to mouth

⭐

Vaccine Content — What's Inside?

  • Suspending fluid: Sterile water or saline
  • Preservatives: For multidose vials — prevent contamination with repeated entry. Thimerosal was removed (1999); no thimerosal in PH vaccines
  • Stabilizers: Prevent antigen degradation (gelatin, ethanol)
  • Adjuvants: Enhance immune response. Only licensed: aluminum salts

Cold Chain

  • Temperature-controlled supply chain for vaccines
  • Most vaccines: kept cold but NOT frozen
  • If frozen → DISCARD
  • If briefly exposed to 28–30°C → can still be used

Contraindications vs Precautions

  • Contraindication: Do NOT give (increases risk of serious adverse reaction)
  • Precaution: Defer (weigh risk vs benefit). E.g., high fever → defer vaccine
  • Mild illness (cough, colds): NOT a contraindication — proceed with vaccination

4
Fails screening → refer for comprehensive developmental evaluation (NOT diagnosis yet)
5

📝 MCQ Practice (30 Questions)

Click each question to reveal the answer and rationale.

Q1  A term newborn's weight at birth is 3.2 kg. After 2 weeks, which of the following weights is most expected?▾
A 2.8 kg
B 3.0 kg
C 3.2 kg
D 3.5 kg
Answer: C — 3.2 kg (exceeds birth weight)
After 2 weeks, the newborn should EXCEED its birth weight. The 10–12% weight drop occurs in the first week, then by week 2, birth weight is regained and gains ~30 g/day thereafter.
Q2  A mother wants to bond with her newborn. Which behavioral state is BEST for breastfeeding initiation?▾
A Active sleep
B Drowsy
C Quiet Alert
D Fussy
Answer: C — Quiet Alert
The Quiet Alert state is characterized by still body, bright shiny eyes, and maximum attentiveness. This is the golden window for feeding and social bonding.
Q3  At what age does Object Permanence first appear?▾
A 4 months
B 6 months
C 9 months
D 12 months
Answer: C — 9 months
Object permanence (understanding that objects still exist when not seen) emerges at ~9 months and represents the FIRST SIGN OF MEMORY. It coincides with stranger anxiety and improved social communication.
Q4  A 4-year-old boy is asked to copy shapes. Which of the following should he be able to copy?▾
A Circle only
B Circle and triangle
C Cross and square
D Triangle and diamond
Answer: C — Cross and Square (48 months/4 years)
Memory aid: 3 years = Circle, 4 years = Cross + Square, 5 years = Triangle. The triangle comes at 5 years.
Q5  A child with birth weight of 3.0 kg has a current weight of 9.0 kg. What is the MINIMUM age of this child?▾
A 4 months
B 8 months
C 12 months
D 18 months
Answer: C — 12 months (1 year)
Birth weight TRIPLES by 12 months. 3.0 kg × 3 = 9.0 kg. This is a classic high-yield fact.
Q6  The FIRST step in EINC (Unang Yakap) is:▾
A Early skin-to-skin contact
B Immediate and thorough drying
C Properly-timed cord clamping
D Non-separation for breastfeeding
Answer: B — Immediate and thorough drying
Common exam trap! The sequence is: Drying → Skin-to-skin → Cord clamping → Non-separation/breastfeeding. Drying comes FIRST, not skin-to-skin.
Q7  ENBS should ideally be performed when?▾
A Immediately at birth
B After 24 hours of life
C At 48 hours of life
D At 7 days of life
Answer: B — After 24 hours of life
The baby must have ingested milk/protein first for accurate metabolic disorder detection. Doing it too early gives false results. Governed by RA 9288.
Q8  A 15-month-old child does not have a pincer grasp. What is the MOST appropriate next step?▾
A Reassure parents as it is within normal variation
B Refer for comprehensive developmental evaluation
C Repeat assessment in 3 months
D Administer developmental screening only
Answer: B — Refer for comprehensive evaluation
Absence of pincer grasp at 15 months is a fine motor red flag. Pincer grasp should be present by 12 months (neat pincer grasp). This warrants immediate referral.
Q9  Which growth indicator is BEST for assessing acute malnutrition/wasting independent of age?▾
A Weight-for-age
B Length/Height-for-age
C Weight-for-length/height
D BMI-for-age
Answer: C — Weight-for-length/height
WFL/WFH detects wasting (acute malnutrition) and can be used even when exact age is unknown. Length/Height-for-age detects stunting (chronic malnutrition). Weight-for-age detects underweight.
Q10  A child <2 years old cannot lie down for measurement. What do you do?▾
A Measure standing height, subtract 0.7 cm
B Measure standing height, add 0.7 cm
C Estimate from weight-for-age chart
D Defer measurement to next visit
Answer: B — Standing height + add 0.7 cm
Standing height is 0.7 cm LESS than recumbent length. So to convert standing to recumbent: ADD 0.7 cm. To convert recumbent to standing (child ≥2 who can't stand): SUBTRACT 0.7 cm.
Q11  Which vaccine route combination is CORRECT in the Philippines?▾
A BCG → Intramuscular
B MMR → Subcutaneous
C OPV → Intramuscular
D Pentavalent → Intradermal
Answer: B — MMR → Subcutaneous
MMR is a live attenuated vaccine, given SC. BCG = ID (not IM). OPV = Oral (not IM). Pentavalent = IM (not ID).
Q12  An 18-month-old child does not point to show interest or share attention. This is a red flag for:▾
A Global developmental delay
B Autism Spectrum Disorder
C Cerebral palsy
D Intellectual disability
Answer: B — Autism Spectrum Disorder
Absence of protodeclarative pointing (pointing to show interest) at 18 months, combined with absent joint attention at 9 months and absent protoimperative pointing at 15 months, are classic ASD red flags. M-CHAT-R is indicated.
Q13  A newborn's mother is HBsAg positive. What should be done within 12 hours of birth?▾
A Hepatitis B vaccine only
B HBIG only
C Hepatitis B vaccine + HBIG (0.5 mL)
D Defer until confirmatory testing
Answer: C — HBV + HBIG within 12 hours
For HBsAg(+) mothers, both HBV vaccine AND HBIG (0.5 mL) are given within 12 hours. Vaccine alone is insufficient. HBIG not later than 7 days if not immediately available.
Q14  A 9-month-old presents for measles vaccination with mild cough and colds. What is the appropriate management?▾
A Defer until symptoms resolve
B Give antibiotics first
C Proceed with vaccination
D Refer to hospital
Answer: C — Proceed with vaccination
Mild acute illness (mild cough, colds, low-grade fever) is NOT a contraindication. Only severe febrile illness, anaphylaxis to vaccine components, or encephalopathy warrants deferral.
Q15  Standardized developmental screening is recommended by PPS at which ages?▾
A 3, 6, 9, 12 months
B 6, 12, 18, 24 months
C 9, 18, 24 (or 30) months + yearly
D Yearly starting at birth
Answer: C — 9, 18, 24 (or 30) months, then yearly
These specific ages are chosen because key developmental milestones and early signs of ASD can be detected. At 18 and 24 months, ASD-specific screening (M-CHAT-R) should also be administered.
Q16  A toddler uses a banana as a telephone during play. This is an example of:▾
A Symbolic transformation (18–24 months)
B Egocentric symbolic play (12–18 months)
C Associate play
D Pre-operational magical thinking
Answer: B — Egocentric Symbolic Play (12–18 months)
Using objects for their intended purpose relative to the child's OWN body (e.g., comb for own hair, banana as telephone) = egocentric symbolic play at 12–18 months. When play extends to OTHERS (e.g., feeding doll) = symbolic transformation at 18–24 months.
Q17  Blood pressure monitoring in children should begin annually at what age?▾
A 1 year
B 2 years
C 3 years
D 5 years
Answer: C — 3 years
Annual blood pressure monitoring begins at age 3 or earlier for high-risk conditions. High-risk conditions include prematurity, low birth weight, congenital heart disease, renal disease, and obesity.
Q18  A child's Rotavirus vaccine last dose was NOT administered beyond the recommended maximum age. What is the main reason for this strict age limit?▾
A Increased risk of anaphylaxis
B Decreased immune response
C Risk of intussusception
D Risk of febrile seizures
Answer: C — Risk of intussusception
Rotavirus vaccine has a strict maximum age limit due to increased risk of intussusception in older infants. Also contraindicated in children with SCID or history of intussusception. RV1: last dose not beyond 24 weeks. RV5 (liquid): not beyond 32 weeks.
Q19  A 3-year-old can ride a tricycle, knows his age and sex, counts 3 objects, and most of his speech is intelligible to strangers. What finding would be a RED FLAG at this age?▾
A Cannot copy a square
B Cannot hop on one foot
C Cannot use 3-word sentences
D Cannot name 4 colors
Answer: C — Cannot use 3-word sentences
By 3 years, children should use 3-word sentences (rule of thumb: words in sentence = age). Inability to use 3-word sentences at 3 years is a language red flag. Copying a square is a 4-year milestone. Hopping is a 4-year milestone. Naming 4 colors is a 5-year milestone.
Q20  Which of the following is a SERIOUS AEFI that must be reported within 24–48 hours?▾
A Intussusception
B Lymphadenitis
C Hypotonic-Hyporesponsive Episode (HHE)
D Thrombocytopenia
Answer: C — HHE
The 24–48 hour AEFIs are: Anaphylaxis, Anaphylactoid reaction, Persistent inconsolable screaming >3 hrs, HHE, and Toxic Shock Syndrome. Intussusception and thrombocytopenia are within 3 months. Lymphadenitis is 1–12 months (BCG-related).
Q21  In the Philippines, why are pentavalent vaccines given at 6, 10, 14 weeks instead of 2, 4, 6 months like in the US?▾
A Philippine infants have weaker immune systems
B To reduce adverse effects
C To prevent disease early and reduce loss to follow-up
D Cost is lower with earlier vaccination
Answer: C — Prevent disease early and reduce loss to follow-up
From a public health perspective, PH uses the minimum safe interval (4 weeks) to complete vaccination faster (~4 months vs ~6 months in US), reducing loss to follow-up.
Q22  A 5-year-old's immunizations were interrupted at age 2. The next step is:▾
A Restart the entire vaccine series from the beginning
B Continue (catch-up) from where the series was interrupted
C Wait until adolescence to restart
D Give only the most recent age-appropriate vaccines
Answer: B — Continue/catch-up from where interrupted
A fundamental rule in vaccinology: interruption NEVER requires restarting the series. The immune system "remembers" prior doses. Always use catch-up schedules for delayed vaccinations.
Q23  An infant received MMR at 9 months due to limited vaccine availability. At 12 months, they receive MMR again. Which statement is TRUE?▾
A The 9-month dose counts as dose 1 of the MMR series
B The 9-month dose does NOT count; the 12-month dose is dose 1
C Two doses have now been completed
D No more doses needed
Answer: B — The 9-month dose does NOT count
Measles (or MMR) given at 9 months is for outbreak/emergency use but does NOT count as part of the routine MMR series. The child still needs 2 scheduled MMR doses at the recommended ages (12 months and 4–6 years).
Q24  Parents refuse vaccination due to fear after a vaccine controversy. The most appropriate physician response is:▾
A Report parents to authorities
B Vaccinate the child against parents' wishes
C Provide evidence-based information and address concerns
D Inform them vaccines are mandated by the government
Answer: C — Provide evidence-based information
The most ethical approach is to build trust through autonomy and informed consent using evidence-based data, while maintaining the physician-patient relationship. Coercion is not appropriate.
Q25  Which is a unique feature of the Philippine NIP compared to the US immunization program?▾
A Use of IPV only
B BCG vaccination
C MMR at 12 months
D RSV vaccine at birth
Answer: B — BCG vaccination
BCG, Typhoid, Cholera, Rabies, and JEV are unique to the Philippine schedule due to local disease endemicity. The US does not routinely use OPV (only IPV) and includes RSV, COVID-19, Dengue, Mpox vaccines instead.
Q26  A mother asks when to schedule her child's first dental visit. You advise:▾
A At 3 years old
B At 2 years old when all teeth erupt
C When the first tooth erupts (~6 months), no later than 12 months
D At 18 months
Answer: C — First tooth eruption (~6 months), no later than 12 months
First dental visit should coincide with first tooth eruption and absolutely no later than 12 months. All 20 primary teeth should erupt by 3 years old.
Q27  A 2-year-old child is asked to point at a picture of a dog, and does so correctly. At 19 months, parents noted 2-word phrases. Which of the following would still be expected at this age?▾
A Ability to count 3 objects
B Ability to hop on one foot
C Understanding 2-step commands
D Knowing age and sex
Answer: C — Understanding 2-step commands (24 months)
At 24 months: 2-step commands (receptive), 2-word sentences (expressive). Counting 3 objects = 36 months. Hopping = 48 months. Knowing age and sex = 36 months.
Q28  Exclusive breastfeeding is recommended until what age?▾
A 2 months
B 4 months
C 6 months
D 12 months
Answer: C — 6 months
Exclusive breastfeeding (no other food or drink) until 6 months, then continued breastfeeding PLUS complementary feeding up to 2 years and beyond (WHO/PPS recommendation).
Q29  A pediatrician observes a child, takes a developmental history, and makes observations without using any standardized tool. This describes:▾
A Developmental screening
B Developmental surveillance
C Diagnostic developmental evaluation
D Early intervention
Answer: B — Developmental surveillance
Surveillance = continuous monitoring at every well-child visit using observation, history, documentation — NO standardized tool. Screening uses validated instruments (PEDS, M-CHAT-R) at specific ages.
Q30  A 6-month-old is receiving IVIG (2 g/kg) for Kawasaki disease. When can MMR be safely administered?▾
A 4 months after IVIG
B 8 months after IVIG
C 11 months after IVIG
D No delay needed
Answer: C — 11 months after IVIG
IVIG (2 g/kg) delays MMR and Varicella by 11 months. Rabies immunoglobulin (RIG) delays MMR/Varicella by 4 months. The concern is that passive antibodies from IVIG will neutralize the live attenuated vaccine.
